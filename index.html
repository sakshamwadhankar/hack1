International Space Station

A permanently crewed orbital laboratory that travels at ~28,000 km/h about 400 km above Earth, enabling groundbreaking research in microgravity.

  • Orbit: Low Earth Orbit (~51.6° inclination, ~92 min period)
  • Altitude: ~400–420 km
  • Speed: ~27,600–28,000 km/h
  • Crew: Typically 6–7 astronauts from international partners
  • Research: Human health, materials science, Earth observation, technology demos

How this tracker works

We use publicly available Two-Line Elements (TLEs) to compute the ISS orbit and render it on a 3D globe. The tracker updates position and orbit path periodically to reflect the latest predictions.
